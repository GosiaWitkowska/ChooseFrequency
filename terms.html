My Responsibilities

Provide service to clients solely in those areas in which I am competent to do so and for which I carry relevant professional indemnity insurance. A copy of my insurance is available on request. “Competency” means adequate training, skills and experience but need not exclude treating a client for a condition which the practitioner has not treated before, provided that due diligence and professionalism is observed.  Act in a non-biased, non-prejudicial manner towards all clients, providing those clients with an identical quality of service and treatment irrespective of the many differences which are to be found between clients, including but not restricted to: race, gender, sexual orientation, disability etc. Disclose full details of all relevant memberships, training, experience, qualifications and appropriate avenues of complaint to clients upon request and only use those qualifications and memberships to which they have proof of entitlement. Explain fully to clients in advance of any treatment: the fee levels, precise terms of payment and any charges which might be imposed for non-attendance or cancelled appointments, and wherever relevant, confidentiality issues. Present all services and products in an unambiguous manner (to include any limitations and realistic outcomes of treatment) and ensure that the client retains complete control over the decision to purchase such services or products.  N.B. Guarantees of either a cure or a successful resolution of the problem/s presented shall not be offered. I will adhere to and uphold the code of ethics of the IAPC&M A copy of this code of ethics is also available to the client at all times.


Your Responsibilities

As the Client, you also have responsibilities in your work with me. First, it is your responsibility to turn up for your sessions. When possible, client sessions are set up on a regular schedule, which is not just for the convenience of my scheduling. The regularity of your sessions is an important part of your "inside" work. When circumstances happen that are beyond your control I will work to find a way round this for you. Your second responsibility is to take seriously the work we are doing – to cooperate with requests to do any "homework" given to you, and to spend time between sessions thinking, exploring and experimenting in the areas we are working on together. You are responsible for being as open and honest with us as is possible. At times this may be your greatest challenge in therapy. And it is my pledge to encourage and honour the specific trust involved in your openness with me. You are expected to be free of the influence of alcohol and drugs, except with the specific knowledge and approval of the therapist.
Below are terms of the contract:

  • The therapy session will last for aproximatly 60 minutes
  • If the client is late for the appointment, the session cannot be extended
  • The first session is used as an opportunity to clarify the client’s reason for coming and to take some personal history and details and can be up to 90 minutes long
  • If you are unable to keep an appointment then please give me a call with at least 24 hours notice
  • The full fee will be charged for missed appointments where there is no notification and for sessions cancelled with less than 24 hours notice
  • The service offered is confidential. Brief notes are kept during therapy but these will be stored separately from your personal details and will be in a secure location
  • Your therapist has regular supervision on a monthly basis
  • If you give me reason to believe that you will cause other people harm or yourself then I will take the appropriate action on advice from my supervisory body.
